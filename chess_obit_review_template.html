About the OBIT Method

Openings

Review and update your opening repertoire to the first move you would have played differently.

Blunders

Analyze game-deciding mistakes and identify common themes in your errors.

Interesting

Examine confusing positions where you spent extra time thinking.

Takeaway

Game Details

Guiding Questions:

  • • Did you follow your prepared lines?
  • • Where did you deviate from theory or your repertoire?
  • • What opening knowledge gaps were exposed?
  • • How can you improve your preparation for this opening?

Guiding Questions:

  • • What type of mistake was it? (Tactical, positional, time pressure)
  • • Did you consider your opponent's threats?
  • • Were you calculating forcing moves properly?
  • • Is this a recurring pattern in your games?

Guiding Questions:

  • • What were the candidate moves you considered?
  • • What key factors did you miss in your evaluation?
  • • How could you have approached this position differently?
  • • What similar positions should you study?

Guiding Questions:

  • • What is the most important pattern or concept you learned?
  • • How will you prevent similar mistakes in future games?
  • • What aspect of your game needs the most attention?
  • • What will you study or practice next?
